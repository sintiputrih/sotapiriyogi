Jess Lockwood Biography

Jess Lockwood is an American Bull rider who was born and brought up in Volborg, Montana. He is also a rodeo cowboy who competes in the Professional Bull Riders (PBR) tour. He is the youngest Professional Bull Riders circuit’s trophy holder. He won his second PBR world championship in 2019 and became the youngest bull rider with two world championships.

Jess Lockwood Injury

Lockwood missed the rest of the regular season with a right shoulder injury. It is known that he suffered a sprain in his shoulder. The injury occurred when he landed on his right elbow with his arm away from his body after he was tossed from his first-round bull in Lincoln, Nebraska in 2020.

Jess didn’t compete in Lincoln the following day and wasn’t entered in Tulsa. On one occasion, he accidentally released the rope at the end of his ride. Afterward, he crashed into the arena floor head first and had a potential neck-breaking landing.

As a result, Lockwood was diagnosed with a cervical sprain. He also had a groin injury which caused him to limp from the arena after riding Power Puff for 70 points. Therefore, he was unable to compete in the remainder of the event until the PBR World Finals in  2018.

Jess Lockwood House

Lockwood met his mentor, Cody Lambert at 18 years of age while launching his career on the PBR tour. Citing this, he moved from his family’s 10,000-acre ranch in Montana to Lambert’s 200-acre ranch in Texas.

This shift was so that Jess could soak up the collective wisdom of Cody, Justin McBride, J.W. Hart, and Ross Coleman. He stayed there for the first few years of his career until he bought his own ranch in Montana where he lives today.
