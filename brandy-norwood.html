Brandy Norwood is a singer-songwriter, and actress from Mississippi, United States. Being the sister of famous singer-actor, Ray J, she was influenced by music from a young age. She has released several albums Never Say Never, Two Eleven, Afrodisiac, and many others. She has also appeared in the Broadway and off-Broadway musical Chicago from 2015 to 2017. She is also related to wrestler Sasha Banks and singer Snoop Dogg.

    Brandy Norwood Facts

  • In 1996, she was selected by People Magazine in their list featuring 50 Most Beautiful People in the World.
  • The role she eventually played in I Still Know What You Did Last Summer was meant for a Caucasian girl. But after she auditioned for the role, the movie producers decided to cast her.
  • After she was cast in the role of Cinderella in the TV film with the similar name, she became the first African American actress to be cast in the iconic role. She was specially selected by the producer Whitney Houston.
  • Brandy decided that she wanted to become an actress when she was 4 years old. She managed to record her first single by the time she turned 11.
  • In February 2006, she was placed at the 37th spot by VH-1 in their list featuring 100 Greatest Teen Stars. She also managed to grab the 33rd position in their 50 Greatest Teen Idols.
  • In 1990, she was signed up by Teaspoon Productions and was made backing vocalist of their R&B boy band, Immature. The company also arranged for her to record a demo tape.
  • After her music career took off, her mother decided to quit her job and managed Norwood’s music commitments.
  • In 1997, she and her family decided to start The Norwood Kids Foundation, which aimed to help disadvantaged youths in Mississippi and Los Angeles through self-help and arts programs.
  • Mattel launched the “Brandy Doll” in 1999.
  • In 2004, she decided to end her 11 years long association with Atlantic Records claiming that she wanted to move on. Atlantic decided to grant her unconditional release.
  • In December 2006, she was involved in a fatal accident that led to the death of 38-year-old Awatef Aboudihaj, who was driving Toyota with which her Range Rover collided. She wasn’t arrested nor charged because of lack of insufficient evidence.
  • In January 2007, Aboudihaj’s parents decided to file a wrongful death lawsuit against Norwood in which they asked for $50 million settlement. They eventually agreed to reach out of the court settlement with Norwood.
  • She was also slapped with a lawsuit by Aboudihaj’s husband. In February 2009, he decided to reject a $1.2 million settlement offer. But he eventually agreed to settle the lawsuit out of the court in November of the same year.
  • As per court documents filed in June 2009, it was revealed that Aboudihaj’s two children received $300,000 each in the settlement after they had filed their separate lawsuit against Norwood.
  • In March 2016, Brandy filed a lawsuit against Chameleon Entertainment Group and its president, Breyon Prescott, after they reportedly refused to allow her to release new albums. However, the lawsuit was thrown out of court due to a particular clause. She later filed another lawsuit a few months later, demanding $270,000 in compensation and a legal declaration that she was contractually freed from the label. In 2017, it was revealed that both parties had reached a settlement.
  • In November 2016, Brandy was honored with the “Lady of Soul Award” at the Soul Train Music Awards. With this, she became the 2nd recipient of this award.
  • In 2019, she received the “BMI President’s Award” at the BMI R&B/Hip-Hop Awards.
  • In July 2020, she released a new studio album titled B7, which debuted at No. 12 on the Billboard 200 chart and No. 2 on the UK R&B Albums chart.
  • In March 2021, Brandy was selected by John Legend as a mentor for the 20th season of the singing competition series The Voice.
  • In March 2022, she released a new song titled Nothing Without You.
  • In October 2022, it was reported that Brandy had been hospitalized after suffering a seizure.
  • In November 2023, she released a new studio album titled Christmas with Brandy.
  • In November 2023, Brandy performed her song Someday at Christmas at the 2023 Macy’s Thanksgiving Day Parade.
