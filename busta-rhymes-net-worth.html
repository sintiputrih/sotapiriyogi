Busta Rhymes’ Career

Music

The rapper’s career started in 1950 when he formed the East Coast hip hop group called The Leaders of the New School. Busta, along with his three bandmates.

The group got increasingly popular because of Rhyme’s unique style of rap and his personality. However, when the rapper started to get more popular than his bandmates, they split.

From 1993 to 1995, Busta featured in many albums of several other rappers.

Finally, in March of 1996, he released his first solo album titled “The Coming.” His single “Woo Hah!! Got You All in Check” was a hit that led him to a new height of success.

Later, in September of 1997, the rapper released his second album, which has hits like “Put Your Hands Where My Eyes Could See” and “Fire It Up.”

His lead single “Gimme Some More” from the album, Extinction Level Event: Final World Front, reached number six n the UK charts in 1999.

Similarly, in the same year, his song “What’s It Gonna Be?!” featuring Janet Jackson charted on top eleven in both UK and the US. The song “Iz They Wildin Wit Us?” became famous because of Busta’s fast rapping style.

In February of 2019, the rapper confirmed the finishing of his new album, which was soon to be released. And finally, in October of 2020, he released the album Extinction Level Event 2: The Wrath of God. The album featured artists like Kendrick Lamar, Rakim, Mariah Carey, Q-Tip, Mary J Blige, MOP, etc.

Movies and Television Shows

In 1993, the rapper played Jawan in the movie “Who’s the Man?“. The movie proved to be the beginning of his acting career.

Since then, he has acted in eleven different movies and nine different television shows.

He acted in movies like, Who’s the Man?, Strapped, Higher Learning, Shaft, Backstage, Finding Forrester, Narc, Halloween: Resurrection, Full Clip, Breaking Point, The Unforgiven, King of the Dancehall.

Similarly, he did voice acting in The Rugrats Movie, Rugrats, The Boondocks, and Big City Greens.

Three Facts About Busta

  • The rapper held the Guinness world record for saying the most syllables in one second in 2000. A little-known rapper from Chicago stole the title from him a few years later.

  • Busta was once called out for being two minutes late to a movie set. The Oscar-winning actor, Sean Connery, who is also known for his punctuality, handed the rapper a late slip for being late to the set. They were filming the movie, Finding Forrester.
