Career

Winning the RJ Hunt

In 2013, Radio Mirchi was looking for a new radio jockey for their Sunday morning shows; so, they organized a competition titled ‘the hottest RJ in Delhi.’ Akanksha, who was still pursuing her Bachelor degree, got to know about this competition from a senior, who was also participating in it. Akanksha, along with 20000 other participants, took part in the contest, and she ended up winning it.

Akanksha Sharma winning the RJ hunt by Radio Mirchi

Akanksha worked at Radio Mirchi for one year before she quit it to complete her degree. While working at Radio Mirchi, she was placed under the tutelage of the reputed radio jockey RJ Naved.

Akanksha Sharma with RJ Naved

The Voice of Shin-chan

In an interview, while talking about her journey of becoming a voice artist, she said,

So, along with college, I did some Radio commercials which also helped in my training for being a voiceover artist – mimicking kid’s voice for Horlicks advertisements and other different voices for various other things on the radio. From there, I got the idea of being a voice-over artist.”

After completing her graduation, Akanksha started working as a freelance voice-over artist. In 2015, she was offered to play the Hindi voice of the fictional character Shin-chan Nohara in the superhit Japanese animated series Crayon Shin-chan. According to her, she was unaware of the cartoon until she started preparing for its voice. Akanksha is the fourth artist to give the Hindi voice of Shin-chan since it first aired in 2006 in India.

Other Voice-overs

Apart from Shin-chan, she has given her voice for other well-known animated characters like Bo-chan (Shin-chan’s friend), Nobita (from the anime series Doraemon), and Doraemon’s sister, Dorami.

Akanksha is known to have worked for the professional wrestling show WWE where she dubbed the voice of female wrestlers in Hindi.

Akanksha Sharma dubbing for the show WWE

Akanksha can perform more than 120 different cartoon voices.

As a YouTuber

Akanksha started her YouTube channel in 2016, and soon, she became a popular face in the Indian YouTube community. Her subscriber count on YouTube is close to 550K as of 2020. She has uploaded multiple videos on her YouTube channel, which have millions of views. Her most viewed video is titled “Cartoon Voice Mimicry Challenge by Akanksha Sharma,” which has more than 15 million views.
