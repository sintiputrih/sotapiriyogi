Who Knew? The Peaky Blinders Haircut is This Years Unexpected Style Hero

May 2024 · 9 minute read

In the world of style, surprises are as common as a snip at your local barber shop, but who could have predicted the roaring comeback of the Peaky Blinders haircut? This isn’t just another retro revival; it’s a full-scale style insurgency.

Gone are the days when a trip to the barber was just a routine chore. Now, it’s where you step into a slice of history, emerging with more than just a fresh cut – you’re sporting a piece of cinematic style on your head.

You might think a haircut is just a haircut, but the Peaky Blinders style isn’t your average trim. It’s a statement, a nod to a bygone era, and let’s be honest, a cheeky way to add some vintage rogue to your modern look. And no, you don’t need to be a gangster from a 1920s Birmingham gang to pull it off – though a bit of that rebel attitude wouldn’t hurt.

Here’s the deal: the Peaky Blinders haircut isn’t just sweeping through the streets; it’s taking over style conversations from high-end salons to the buzzing hum of your local barber shop.

The Roots of the ‘Peaky Blinders Haircut’

Let’s turn the clock back a bit.

Picture a bustling, gritty Birmingham in the early 20th century – think about the era. This is where the story of the haircut from Peaky Blinders begins, a style rooted deeply in working-class toughness and simplicity.

Back then, the Peaky Blinders haircut was more than a fashion statement; it was a practical choice. The close-shaven sides and slightly longer top weren’t just about looking sharp. In those days, longer hair could be a liability in a scrap, and let’s face it, the Peaky Blinders weren’t exactly spending their evenings knitting. The style spoke volumes about the wearer – resilient, no-nonsense, and with a touch of defiance.

Now, fast forward to today. When you say Peaky Blinders haircut, it conjures up images of well-groomed men with a taste for vintage flair. It’s fascinating, isn’t it? How a haircut, born out of necessity in the working-class neighborhoods of post-war England, has evolved into a symbol of sharp style and edgy sophistication.

Understanding this evolution isn’t just a trip down memory lane. It’s about appreciating the resilience and adaptability of style. When you sit in your barber’s chair asking for the Peaky Blinders haircut, you’re not just getting a trendy look. You’re wearing a piece of history, a homage to those who lived boldly and weren’t afraid to show their true selves, regardless of society’s norms.

The Resurgence: From Screen to Street

You’ve probably heard of ‘Peaky Blinders,’ the TV show that’s as famous for its gripping drama as it is for its distinctive fashion sense. But did you realize that the show has done more than just entertain us? It’s reshaped the landscape of men’s hairstyles. Yes, you heard that right. The Peaky Blinders haircuts have transitioned from being a mere on-screen statement to a real-world fashion trendsetter. It’s a classic case of life imitating art, or rather, style stealing the show!

How the Show Revived the Haircut

So, what’s the secret sauce behind this resurgence? Well, it’s all about the raw, edgy appeal that the show’s characters ooze. They’re not just strutting around in well-tailored suits; they’ve got haircuts that scream boldness and rebellion. The Peaky Blinders haircuts, with their shaved sides and textured tops, have become synonymous with a gritty, no-nonsense attitude that’s strangely alluring.

Celebrity Endorsement

From Hollywood A-listers to sports icons, many have been spotted flaunting Peaky Blinders-inspired hairdos. These stars, known for their keen fashion sense, have given the haircut a stamp of approval, elevating it from a period-piece accessory to a modern style statement.

Think about it – when a celebrity known for their style game sports a Peaky Blinders haircut at a red-carpet event, it sends a message. It’s not just about looking good; it’s about embracing a style that’s bold and unconventional. This celebrity influence has played a pivotal role in transforming the perception of Peaky Blinders haircuts, morphing them from a screen relic to a barber shop favorite.

The Role of Social Media

And let’s not forget the power of social media in this resurgence. Instagram and Pinterest are awash with tutorials, pictures, and posts about the Peaky Blinders hair style. Style influencers and bloggers are dissecting every aspect of the cut, from how to ask your barber for it to how to style it for different occasions. It’s not just a haircut; it’s a lifestyle choice that’s being shared, liked, and followed.

What Makes the Cut Unique?

The Peaky Blinders hairstyle is not just a trip down memory lane; it’s a style statement that’s been turning heads faster than a new sign at your  local barber shop.

The Signature Style

The Peaky Blinders haircut, a blend of old-school charm and modern ruggedness, stands out distinctly from the typical short haircuts flooding your Instagram feed. At its core, it’s characterized by its stark contrasts. Think of a tightly cropped back and sides – we’re talking almost skin-shaved here – paired with a significantly longer, textured top. This isn’t your run-of-the-mill crew cut; it’s a haircut with attitude.

The Variations

Now, don’t think this style is a one-size-fits-all deal. There are variations aplenty. Some go for a more subtle fade on the sides, while others embrace a more dramatic, almost disconnected look. The top part? It can range from a neatly combed back to a more tousled, windswept style. It’s like your favorite coffee; everyone has their unique twist on it.

We chatted with a few pros from the local barber shop scene. And guess what? Achieving the perfect Peaky Blinders hairstyle is less about following a strict template and more about tailoring it to your individual style and hair type. It’s an art, really. The key lies in the transition between the short and long sections – it needs to be seamless yet pronounced.

It’s All in the Details

Here’s where things get interesting. According to the stylists we spoke with, the secret sauce of this cut is precision. Every snip counts. The back and sides need to be meticulously faded to avoid any abrupt lines, and the top requires skillful texturing for that signature Peaky Blinders look.

How to Pull Off the Peaky Blinders Look

So, you’ve decided to embrace the Peaky Blinders haircut style. First things first, let’s talk about getting that cut just right. Walking into your local salon or barber shop, you want to be armed with more than just the name of the cut.

Here’s how you can ensure you and your hairstylist are on the same page:

  • Be Specific: Instead of just saying “Give me the Peaky Blinders hair cut,” bring a photo. This visual aid isn’t just for Instagram; it’s a foolproof way to show exactly what you’re aiming for.
  • Describe the Details: Talk about the specifics – the severe, short back and sides, the longer, textured top. Remember, the devil is in the details, especially when it comes to hair.
  • Discuss Your Hair Type: Your hair might not be a carbon copy of Cillian Murphy’s. Discuss how the Peaky Blinders haircut can be adapted to suit your hair type and face shape. Trust your stylist’s expertise to tailor the cut for you.
  • Set the Expectations: Be clear about how much maintenance you’re willing to put in. This style requires upkeep, so make sure you’re ready for the commitment.

    • Maintaining the Look

    Congratulations, you’ve got the cut! Now, let’s talk about keeping it sharp.

    The Peaky Blinders haircut style isn’t a ‘wake-up-and-go’ kind; it demands attention and care.

  • Regular Trims: To maintain the sharpness of the cut, regular visits to the barber are non-negotiable. Depending on how quickly your hair grows, you might be looking at a trim every few weeks.
  • The Right Products: Invest in a good pomade or wax. You want something that provides hold and texture without weighing your hair down. Ask your stylist for recommendations based on your hair type.
  • Styling Like a Pro: When styling, work a small amount of product into damp hair. Use a comb for a sleek look or your fingers for a more textured style. Remember, less is more – you can always add more product, but starting with a dollop the size of a pea is a good rule of thumb.
  • Daily Care: Keep your hair clean, but don’t over-wash. Too much shampoo can strip natural oils, leaving your hair dry and difficult to style. Consider a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo for regular use.
