Career

Dana White, in 2001, while working as a manager of Chuck Liddell and Titi Ortiz, an opportunity availed itself that would change Dana’s life forever. The company that owned UFC wanted to sell the UFC and Dana appealed to his childhood friend Lorenzo Fertitta to buy the company which he and his brother Frank agreed for $2 million and in exchange let him become the president of the company.

The company struggled a lot to make profits since the government, sponsors, and television executives deemed the sport to be too controversial, and that led to major athletic commissions refusing to sanction the sport. Their troubles didn’t end there as the sport was banned in pay per view and it nearly went dead.

However, in the face of extinction, Dana gave the sport a clean image, and he embarked on a mission to get it approved national wide. Things like strict medical examinations were introduced, kicks at the spine and back of the head were burned, and many more rules that emphasized participants’ safety were introduced. He won the battle with athletic commissions and even with Cable companies. The UFC image was completely transformed, and the MMA was elevated to the mainstream, all thanks to Dana White.

Today UFC, on average, generates around $200 million on PPV revenue and its worth over $1 billion in value.
