Jarid Altmark On Netflix’s Is It Cake Too?

Netflix’s Is It Cake Too? Season 1 was amazing! Viewers and even the judges couldn’t differentiate a cake bucket hat, conch shell, or even a sneaker from the real one. Obviously, the show was a hit. That’s why it’s back for its second installment with a brand new set of talented chefs including our star Jarid Altmark.

Ready to make you question your reality like never before, this fresh batch of bakers who specialize in hyperrealistic bakes will be tasked once again to make the most unlikely objects out of the cake. Think baseball gloves, weekender bags, toilets, and even the Mona Lisa herself — with the hopes of deceiving a slew of seasoned chefs and celebrity guests, and also bagging a share of a $120,000 prize pot.

As for Jarid, he was happier about being able to share his grandmother with the world, 11 years after her death. Presenting the viewers and the judges with a pot that reminded him of her, he said, “Her infectious laugh, sneezes in nines. Resilient cast iron with the graciousness of a grandma as bold as Mrs.Dash seasoning. I remember it in her office, bottom left cabinet next to the stove, almost as vividly as the obnoxious floral breath that’s weaved into every memory like the yarn of my afghan.”

Also, Jarid confessed that he never loved his grandmother as he does now.

Joining Jarid for the second season of Is It Cake Too? were his co-stars Corterrius Allen, Danya Smith, Elizabeth Rowe, Justin Salinas, Kayla Giddings, Liz Marek, Miko Kaw Hok Uy, Pete Tidwell, and Spirit Wallace.

Did you know: In 2018, Jarid applied and competed in Halloween Wars (Season 9) as the youngest competitor to ever compete? 

Jarid Altmark Job

Jarid Altmark is a multi-award-winning cake artist who has been making cakes since he was 12 years old. 

Falling in love with the artistry behind craft-watching shows like Ace of Cakes, Food Network Challenge, and Cake Boss, Jarid competed in his first cake competition at the age of 14 and also founded Jarid’s Awesome Cakes with the help of his parents.

Since then, he’s won several awards in competitions at the student and professional levels and has been featured in several collaborations and magazines such as American Cake Decorating Magazine, Cake Masters Magazine, UCF Today, Entertainment Weekly, and WESH2 News.

Today, Jarid’s Awesome Cakes offers classes for aspiring bakers and several buyable tutorials/recipes. His Awesome Cakes creates whimsical realism that is always cutting-edge and innovative.

Furthermore, this talented baker also travels the country teaching his craft and judging several professional sugar art competitions including Soflo Cake and Candy Expo, The Cake Bar Cake Show, and The Savannah Gingerbread House Competition–mentored by Chef Nicholas Lodge.

When Jarid’s not baking, he works as a communications specialist at TradeStation, a job he received thanks to his Bachelor’s degree in Public Relations from the University of Central Florida. Before this, he worked as a Retail Associate, Key Holder, Marketing Specialist, and Instructor at The Sweet Chalet Shoppe and interned at Central Florida Community Art.

Also, he runs Bored & Baked, a lifestyle and content brand that sheds light and purpose on “the couch-locked munchie eaters that live among us.” Here’s Bored & Baked TikTok @boredandbaked.

Overall, Jarid’s skilled in culinary arts, photography, graphic design, drawing, sculpting, and digital marketing. “I have a passion for storytelling, the entertainment industry, communications, branding, and production,” he said.
