Top 5 DOA: Your Favorite Rappers Share Their Favorite Rapper

January 2024 · 7 minute read

In hip-hop culture, GOAT status is highly coveted. Fans will go to lengths unseen to defend the honor of their favorite artists, whether in real life or on the internet. In such a competitive scene, the discussion of "top five dead or alive" remains a recurring one, and has been throughout recent history. Everybody has their own opinion, including the rappers we all respect and listen to.

So who's the greatest of all time? We've looked back on responses and opinions from some legends of the game, and gathered a diverse array of nominations. But the discussion doesn't end there. Leave your own favorite rappers in the comment section. Make your case, or simply discover something new.

Photo credit: Kristian Dowling / Getty Images


Eminem

Top 5 DOA: Your Favorite Rappers Share Their Favorite Rapper

Year: 2002 / 2013

Picks: (2002) Redman, Jay-Z, 2Pac, Biggie, Andre 3000, Jada, Kurupt, Nas, Himself / (2013) LL, Run DMC, Beastie Boys, Kool G Rap, Big Daddy Kane, Treach

In his 2002 hit, "Till I Collapse," Em famously rattles off his top rappers. Years later, he revisited the subject of his favorite rappers with Elite Daily, giving the shine on his formative influences:

 “I don’t know if I can put an order on it. I would probably say all time… LL would have to be in there….There’s too many I wanna throw up in there. Run DMC, Beastie Boys, Kool G Rap, Big Daddy Kane, Treach…”

Kendrick Lamar

Top 5 DOA: Your Favorite Rappers Share Their Favorite Rapper

Year: 2012

Picks: 2pac, Biggie, Jay-Z, Nas, Eminem

"The emotion (2Pac) put, and how much he related to the people. How much he connected, I think that's what music is about. Music supposed to move. Biggie, because his wit. Biggie's wit was incredible, his character, his personality and his flow, and most of all his confidence. Jay-Z - it's an incredible thing where you can actually listen to his music and feel like you're having a sit down with him, but he's connecting and rhyming these words in the craziest ways. Nas - the art of storytelling. Eminem just been a wordsmith - an amazing wordsmith."
